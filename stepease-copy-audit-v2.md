## SECTION 1 — Hero

### What to Change and Why

**Pre-headline**
The current "Stop writing SOPs manually" is a command, not empathy. It skips the nodding moment — the user hasn't been given a reason to trust you yet. Replace it with something that mirrors what the user already believes.

| | Copy |
|---|---|
| ❌ Current | Stop writing SOPs manually |
| ✅ Replace with | Your team's knowledge lives in people's heads. That's a risk. |

---

**Headline**
The current "Build Professional SOPs in Minutes, Not Weeks" leads with output, not pain. It tells users what the product does before making them feel the problem it solves. The new headline targets exhaustion — which is ongoing and felt right now — rather than time savings, which is abstract.

| | Copy |
|---|---|
| ❌ Current | Build Professional SOPs in Minutes, Not Weeks |
| ✅ Replace with | Stop explaining things over and over. |

**Why this headline wins over the other options discussed:**
- Speaks to the *person*, not the product
- Targets exhaustion (ongoing) vs. frustration (momentary) or fear (abstract)
- Creates an instant nod — the user feels understood before they read anything else
- "Over and over" has rhythm that sounds like the exhaustion it describes
- 5 words — punchy on mobile, reads in under 2 seconds

---

**Subheadline**
The current subheadline has three jobs: explain the mechanism, promise the outcome, and provide social proof. None of them land because they're competing. The social proof ("Trusted by 500+ operations teams") especially weakens the line — social proof in a subheadline signals insecurity. The stats block below handles that job. Remove it from here.

| | Copy |
|---|---|
| ❌ Current | Tell AI about your process like you'd explain it to a colleague. Get a polished, compliance-ready SOP in under 10 minutes. Trusted by 500+ operations teams. |
| ✅ Replace with | Describe your process out loud. Get a polished, audit-ready SOP in under 10 minutes. |

Two sentences. One job each: mechanism, then outcome. "Audit-ready" is stronger than "compliance-ready" — it implies the SOP will hold up under scrutiny, which is the real anxiety for operations and quality teams.

---

**Primary CTA**
The "10 minutes" claim is your biggest value prop. It currently lives in the subhead. It should be on the button — the action trigger — where it does the most work.

| | Copy |
|---|---|
| ❌ Current | Create Your First SOP Free |
| ✅ Replace with | Build Your First SOP Free — Takes 10 Minutes |

---

**Where to put each element**
- Pre-headline: Small caps or muted color, 14–16px, sits directly above the headline
- Headline: Largest text on the page — nothing should compete with it visually
- Subheadline: Max 2 lines at desktop width. If it wraps to 3, cut it.
- Primary CTA: Visible without scrolling on all devices. Full-width on mobile.
- Stats block (47,892 SOPs, 500+ teams, etc.): Keep exactly where it is — below the fold, after the emotional hook has landed first

## SECTION 2 — Features

### What to Change and Why

**Section headline**
"Your Documentation, Finally Done Right" is vague. "Done right" compared to what? It doesn't name the problem it solves.

| | Copy |
|---|---|
| ❌ Current | Your Documentation, Finally Done Right |
| ✅ Replace with | Everything Your Team Needs to Stop Losing Knowledge |

"Stop Losing Knowledge" names the actual problem: when a key employee leaves, when a new hire guesses instead of follows process, when an audit finds inconsistencies. That's knowledge loss. Naming it makes the feature section feel like it's solving something real.

**Section subheadline**
This is one of the strongest lines on the page. The "nothing you don't" contrast is clean and confident.

| | Copy |
|---|---|
| ✅ Keep as-is | Everything you need to eliminate documentation debt. Nothing you don't. |

---

### Feature Card Titles — What to Change and Why

**Card 1 — "Just Talk, We'll Write"**
Strongest card title on the page. Conversational, clear, implies ease without claiming it.

| | Copy |
|---|---|
| ✅ Keep as-is | Just Talk, We'll Write |

---

**Card 2 — "Fix Outdated Docs Fast"**
"Fast" is the weakest possible adjective — generic and empty. "Fix" also implies manual work, which contradicts the product's core promise.

| | Copy |
|---|---|
| ❌ Current | Fix Outdated Docs Fast |
| ✅ Replace with | Kill Outdated Docs Before They Kill a Process |

Outdated SOPs don't just waste time — they cause incidents. "Kill a Process" is deliberately strong to make that consequence stick.

---

**Card 3 — "Universal Export"**
Sounds like a settings menu option. Describes the mechanism, not the value.

| | Copy |
|---|---|
| ❌ Current | Universal Export |
| ✅ Replace with | One Click. Every Format. |

---

**Card 4 — "Activity & Version Control"**
GitHub terminology. Means nothing emotional to an operations manager or HR lead. Sounds like a developer feature, not a compliance safety net.

| | Copy |
|---|---|
| ❌ Current | Activity & Version Control |
| ✅ Replace with | Full Audit Trail. No Excuses in a Review. |

The real value isn't the feature — it's what it protects them from: being in a review meeting and not knowing which version of a procedure was active on a given date.

---

**Card 5 — "Chat-Based Revisions"**
"Chat-Based" describes the mechanism. Users don't care how they revise — they care that they don't have to rewrite the whole document manually.

| | Copy |
|---|---|
| ❌ Current | Chat-Based Revisions |
| ✅ Replace with | Tell AI What Changed. It'll Handle the Rest. |

---

**Card 6 — "Public Viewing & Sharing"**
Flattest title on the page. "Public" can also sound alarming in a compliance context.

| | Copy |
|---|---|

| ✅ Replace with | Share Instantly. No Login Required. |

## SECTION 3 — How It Works

### What to Change and Why

**Section headline**
"Four steps to perfect documentation" repeats the hero's value prop and "perfect" is a claim users don't believe. This section headline should make the process feel surprisingly simple.

| | Copy |
|---|---|
| ❌ Current | Four steps to perfect documentation |
| ✅ Replace with | Four Questions. One Professional SOP. |

The period between the two fragments creates a pause that implies simplicity. Users who've spent weeks wrestling with SOP templates will find this genuinely surprising.

## SECTION 4 — Use Cases

### What to Change and Why

**Section headline**
"Built for every team" resonates with no one. It's the headline you write when you're afraid to exclude anyone — but the result is that it speaks to nobody specifically.

Two options were discussed — neither is wrong, choose based on tone preference:

| Option | Copy | Tone |
|---|---|---|
| ❌ Current | Built for every team | Generic |
| ✅ Option A | Your Industry Has SOPs. We Help You Actually Write Them. | Direct, slightly challenging |
| ✅ Option B | Wherever Processes Get Messy, Stepease Cleans Them Up | Warmer, broader |

---

### Use Case Card Descriptions — What to Change and Why

The current descriptions read like a brochure. They describe what Stepease does rather than naming the pain each team actually lives with.

---

**Operations Teams**

| | Copy |
|---|---|
| ❌ Current | Standardize workflows across departments and locations. |
| ✅ Replace with | Stop each location doing things their own way. |

"Standardize" is your solution word. "Stop each location doing things their own way" is the problem word. Lead with the problem.

---

**Compliance & Quality**

| | Copy |
|---|---|
| ❌ Current | Meet audit requirements with properly documented procedures. |
| ✅ Replace with | Walk into any audit with documented procedures and a full version history. No scrambling. |

"No scrambling" is the emotional payoff. The compliance manager has scrambled before an audit. Two words naming that experience are worth a paragraph.

---

**HR & Training**

| | Copy |
|---|---|
| ❌ Current | Create onboarding materials that scale with your team. |
| ✅ Replace with | New hire on Monday? Your onboarding SOP can be ready by Friday. No rewriting for every role. |

"Scale with your team" is abstract. "Ready by Friday" is a concrete deadline they believe.

---

**IT & Security**

| | Copy |
|---|---|
| ❌ Current | Document incident response and security protocols. |

IT security is one of the few contexts where fear is the right lever. Name the moment they're trying to avoid.

## SECTION 5 — Testimonials

### The Problem
The quote itself is strong — specific numbers (3 weeks → 4 hours), credible role, qualitative detail. But one testimonial with an initial avatar instead of a real photo creates a trust problem. The user's brain does this automatically:

> One testimonial = cherry-picked
> No photo = can't verify this is real
> Therefore = probably made up

This is worse than having no testimonials at all.

### Three Fix Options Discussed

**Option A — Expand to 3+ testimonials**
Add two more with the same structure: specific numbers + qualitative observation + real role title. Three testimonials with initials is more credible than one. Use a horizontal scrolling carousel on mobile.

**Option B — Replace with a metric-forward trust strip** *(recommended for now)*
```
47,892 SOPs created · Average creation time: 9 minutes · 500+ teams
```
Numbers at this scale are harder to dismiss than a single unverified quote. No name or photo required.

**Option C — Reframe as a before/after micro case study**
```
Mid-size logistics company
Before: 3-week SOP process, 4 template versions in use, 2 compliance flags
After:  9-minute average creation time, one standard template, 0 flags at last audit
```
The specificity creates credibility without requiring a name or photo.

**Recommendation: Use Option B now. Replace with Option A when you have real photos.**

## SECTION 6 — Pricing

### What to Change and Why

**The "$5,000/month consultant" line is in the wrong place.**
Currently buried at the bottom of the page in the closing CTA section, where attention is lowest. This is the strongest anchoring line on the page — it reframes the cost of Stepease against a painful alternative. It should sit directly above the pricing grid, where purchase decisions happen.

| | Copy | Location |
|---|---|---|
| ❌ Current position | The power of a $5,000/month consultant — for free. | Bottom CTA section |
| ✅ Move to above pricing grid | The power of a $5,000/month documentation consultant — for $0 to start. | Directly above pricing grid |

## SECTION 7 — Bottom CTA

### What to Change and Why

**Headline**
"Ready to Reclaim Your Week?" is one of the most commonly used SaaS closing headlines. The question format also creates micro-resistance — the user has to internally answer before proceeding. End with a statement, not a question.

| | Copy |
|---|---|
| ❌ Current | Ready to Reclaim Your Week? |
| ✅ Replace with | Your next SOP is 10 minutes away. |

Specific. Time-bound. Confident. Closes the loop on the core promise made in the hero.
